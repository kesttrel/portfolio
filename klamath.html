Undamming the Klamath Painting

Media: watercolor, colored pencil, and acrylic pens

This painting is in celebration of the legislation passed in the fall of 2020 to un-dam the Klamath River. It serves to honor the communities who fought for this change for decades, namely Indigenous communities, led in large part by Indigenous women.

The Klamath and surrounding connected rivers are ancestral land to many tribes, locally to the Hoopa and Yurok Tribes. This painting honors activists like Chief Caleen Sisk of the Winnemem Wintu Tribe located upriver in Northern California who has led the Salmon Runs. Un-damming the Klamath along with other restoration efforts is a huge step in resorting the ney-puy (salmon, Yurok) populations, which are foundational to food sovereignty of the Yurok Tribe among many others. I chose to paint animals and plants that are significant to the Yurok Tribe such as ney-puy (salmon), puuek (deer), mey-weehl (elk), ley-chehl (berries) and keehl (redwood trees).

I borrow this idea from Professor Growing Thunder of the UC Davis Native American Studies department, that healing the relationship to land will in turn allow healing within past and present colonial based trauma in relation to Indigenous women and communities.
